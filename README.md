# Introduction

Cap is a credit platform backed by financial guarantees, where each loan is underwritten with escrowed collateral to secure the Lender.

The platform relies on a market of Underwriters to independently originate and insure USD loans out of its reserve. Underwriters put their own capital behind each delegation decision, making honest underwriting the dominant strategy. In return, Underwriters receive an underwriting premium from credit spreads. Lenders earn a secured yield that is insured by Underwriter collateral, with all risk coverage transparent and enforced by smart contracts.

Cap issues two products:

* **cUSD** is a digital dollar issued on the Ethereum blockchain. cUSD's reserve is backed by regulated, dollar-denominated stablecoins such as USDC and WTGXX — issued by institutions with transparent attestations. It is redeemable 1:1 for any of the available reserve assets. As more institutions issue their own stablecoins, cUSD serves as the neutral aggregation layer, offering singleness of money across the reserve.
* **stcUSD** is a yield-bearing savings product issued by staking cUSD. Yield is generated via an open network of Borrowers whose strategies are underwritten by Underwriter collateral. Lenders have full downside protection — if a Borrower defaults, the Underwriter's escrowed collateral is liquidated before Lenders absorb any loss. All guarantees are verifiable onchain.
